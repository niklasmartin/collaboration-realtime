V2 dark variants — pick a direction

Review artifact, not part of the article. Three dark-canvas takes on "request-response, then an agent enters" plus a fourth "shared canvas" direction where both participants author nodes onto one graph — same content, with the Miro-style proposal+parallel beat added.

Variant A — Swimlanes

Provenance by position: the agent's proposal is born in a narrow lane, physically apart from the human's spine, then slides on and commits once approved.

Variant B — Signed

Provenance by attribution: every node on the single spine carries a persistent authorship stamp — "you" or "Research agent" — that accumulates as the spine builds and flips on approval.

Variant C — Assembly line

The control: a single spine, each element dropping in beneath the last, no lanes and no stamps — only the cursor tags carry identity.

Variant D — Shared canvas

The Miro-style direction: a simplified graph canvas where both you and the agent author nodes onto one shared surface. The signature beat is parallel authorship — you type your own solution while the agent is concurrently thinking up its own; your node then slides left to make room for the agent's dashed sibling. Research nests *inside* your solution card as an indented sub-line, because it belongs there, not as a sibling on the canvas.
